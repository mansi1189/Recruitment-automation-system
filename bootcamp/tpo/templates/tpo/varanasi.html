How to reach Indore

Indore is the cultural capital of India and the melting pot of Indian civilization. Indore or Benaras has a well-developed transport network and is well connected to all the major Indian cities and states by air, road and rail.

By Air

Indore is well connected and accessible to major Indian cities and tourist spots. There are daily domestic flights to and from Indore to several cities in India. Apart from the state owned Indian Airlines, there are many private air taxi operators that offer their services from Indore to other Indian cities. In fact, the daily flights on Delhi-Agra-Khajuraho-Indore route are quite popular among the tourists.

By Rail

Since Indore lies in the heartland of the North Indian plains, it is well connected to Delhi, Kolkata, Mumbai and other parts of India. There are two railway stations in Indore, the Kashi Junction and the Indore Junction (also known as Indore Cantonment). Rajdhani Express from Delhi or from Calcutta passes through Indore too. One can also catch trains from Mughalsarai, just 10 km south of Indore.

By Road

Situated in the flat Ganga plains, Indore has a good network of roads. frequent public and private buses and road transport to all the major towns of Uttar Pradesh and nearby areas.
